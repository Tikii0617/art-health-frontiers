About the Event

Where Art Meets Science

Across cultures and centuries, music, visual expression and narration have been used not simply as creative productions, but as regulators of physiological states, as mirrors of emotions, and as portals to the creation of meaning.

Our invitation is to explore these mechanisms through an evidence-based lens, while honouring the profoundly subjective nature of art. Our aim is simple yet ambitious: to show that art is not an accessory to health, it is a pathway to it.

By combining subjective experience with objective measurement, we hope to illuminate how transdisciplinary methods can strengthen well-being and inspire future research.

"Art is not an accessory to health — it is a pathway to it."

Programme

Afternoon Schedule

14:00 — 14:15
Welcome & Installation
📍 Learning Center
Reception of participants, orientation to spaces, calm atmosphere with quiet music.
14:15 — 14:30
General Introduction: Art & Health
📍 Learning Center
Institutional context, mental health & planetary health issues, ethical framework and presentation of the afternoon.
👤 Giuliana & Marieve
14:30 — 14:50
WHO International Framework
📍 Learning Center
Art, Health and Planetary Health — international framework, link to the SDGs, social art & field initiatives.
👤 Isabelle Wachsmuth
🌎

Key themes: Art-based interventions as public-health tools that reduce anxiety, depression, trauma and chronic pain, while strengthening resilience and emotional regulation.

🤝

Community approach: Creative and community-driven approaches — Living Museums, storytelling, music, dance, and visual arts — that restore social bonds and collective well-being.

🏥

Health systems: How art complements health systems by humanizing care, improving recovery times, and promoting health equity and inclusive, people-centered care.

14:50 — 15:20
Art Therapy & Integrative Oncology
📍 Learning Center
Clinical feedback, mind-body approaches, art therapy and visualization, key scientific data.
👤 Dr. Laura Nasi
🩺

Mind-body medicine: How imagery and visualization function as active biological regulators rather than supportive add-ons, drawing on psycho-neuro-immunology and systems biology.

🔬

Scientific framework: Impact on perception, emotional regulation, and immune processes — linking clinical practice, scientific evidence, and creative approaches to health.

15:20 — 16:05
Immersive Workshops — Group 1
📍 Learning Extension & Learning Center
Art therapy workshop with music, visual arts, and physical/emotional work. Parallel writing workshop and case study interviews.
👤 Fanie Fabia & Yazied Kei Lanur
🎨

Art Therapy (Fanie Fabia): Explore cosmic art and visual expression while the Visual Analogue Scale (VAS) measures immediate variations in subjective experience before and after the session.

📝

Narrative Workshop (Yazied): An experiential, immersive, and conversational workshop exploring how a work of art that is part of our own life story can promote a "sense of coherence" (Antonovsky) and health factors.

📈

Research component: QR code-based anonymous data collection to evaluate subjective experience before and after the workshops.

16:05 — 17:20
Break & Workshops — Group 2
📍 Lounge & Learning Extension
Sustainable food & drinks break, free exchanges, then second round of art therapy and writing workshops.
👤 Fanie Fabia & Yazied Kei Lanur
☕

Break (16:05–16:35): Sustainable food and drinks in the Lounge, informal exchanges between participants, speakers, and facilitators.

🔄

Group rotation (16:35–17:20): Second round of workshops with group rotation — participants who did art therapy now join writing, and vice versa.

17:20 — 18:00
Results & Collective Artistic Closing
📍 Learning Center & Lounge
Presentation of preliminary results, scientific perspectives, acknowledgements, and a collective closing with a mini-concert celebrating music, movement, and joy.
